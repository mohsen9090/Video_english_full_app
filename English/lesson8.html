Lesson 8: Making Suggestions and Giving Advice

Lesson Objective:

In this lesson, you will learn how to make suggestions and give advice in different situations, as well as how to express agreement or disagreement.

Key Vocabulary:

  • Suggest
  • Advise
  • Should
  • Ought to
  • What about...
  • How about...
  • Why don't you...

Sample Dialogues:

Dialogue 1:

Person A: What should I do if I’m feeling sick?

Person B: You should see a doctor and get some rest.

Person A: I’m also really tired, what about taking a nap?

Person B: Yes, taking a short nap could help you feel better.

Dialogue 2:

Person A: I have a lot of homework to do. I don’t know where to start.

Person B: You should start with the most difficult subjects first.

Person A: Hmm, I’ll try that. Thanks for the advice!

Dialogue 3:

Person A: I think I want to go on a diet.

Person B: You should eat more vegetables and drink plenty of water.

Person A: That’s a good idea! How about exercising too?

Person B: Yes, exercise is important. You should go for a walk every day.

Dialogue 4:

Person A: I’m thinking of quitting my job. I don’t like it anymore.

Person B: You should talk to your manager before making any decision.

Person A: Maybe you're right. I’ll try to have a conversation with him first.

Dialogue 5:

Person A: What do you think about going on vacation next month?

Person B: That’s a great idea! You should definitely take a break.

Person A: How about going to the beach?

Person B: I love the beach! We should book a trip soon.
