Lesson 10: Talking About Family

Lesson Objective:

In this lesson, you will learn how to talk about your family, introduce family members, and ask questions related to family.

Key Vocabulary:

  • Mother
  • Father
  • Brother
  • Sister
  • Grandmother
  • Grandfather
  • Uncle
  • Aunt
  • Parents
  • Children

Sample Dialogues:

Dialogue 1:

Person A: Do you have any siblings?

Person B: Yes, I have one brother and one sister.

Person A: That's nice! What are their names?

Person B: My brother's name is John, and my sister's name is Emily.

Dialogue 2:

Person A: How old is your father?

Person B: My father is 45 years old.

Person A: And how about your mother?

Person B: My mother is 42 years old.

Dialogue 3:

Person A: Do you visit your grandparents often?

Person B: Yes, I visit my grandparents every weekend.

Person A: That sounds wonderful! Where do they live?

Person B: They live in a small house in the countryside.

Dialogue 4:

Person A: How many people are there in your family?

Person B: There are five people in my family.

Person A: Who are they?

Person B: My father, mother, brother, sister, and I.

Dialogue 5:

Person A: Do you have any aunts or uncles?

Person B: Yes, I have two uncles and one aunt.

Person A: What do they do for work?

Person B: My uncles are both teachers, and my aunt is a nurse.
