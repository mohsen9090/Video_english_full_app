Lesson 12: Debating and Expressing Opinions

Lesson Objective:

In this lesson, you will learn how to express opinions, agree or disagree, and engage in debates on various topics.

Key Vocabulary:

  • Opinion
  • Agree
  • Disagree
  • Debate
  • Argument
  • Evidence
  • Point of view
  • Support
  • Oppose
  • Convince

Sample Dialogues:

Dialogue 1:

Person A: Do you think climate change is real?

Person B: Yes, I believe climate change is a real and serious issue that needs immediate action.

Person A: But don't you think it's just part of the Earth's natural cycle?

Person B: No, the scientific evidence shows that human activities, like burning fossil fuels, are contributing to global warming.

Dialogue 2:

Person A: What do you think about online education?

Person B: I think online education is very effective, but it lacks the personal interaction that in-person classes offer.

Person A: I disagree. Online education gives students more flexibility and access to global resources.

Dialogue 3:

Person A: Should governments provide free healthcare for all citizens?

Person B: I strongly agree. Healthcare is a basic human right and should not depend on one's ability to pay.

Dialogue 4:

Person A: Do you think technology is making people less social?

Person B: Yes, I think technology is isolating people more, as they spend more time online than interacting face to face.

Dialogue 5:

Person A: Do you support the idea of electric cars?

Person B: Absolutely! Electric cars are better for the environment and help reduce pollution.
