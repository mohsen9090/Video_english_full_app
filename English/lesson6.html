Lesson 6: Making Suggestions and Giving Advice

Lesson Objective:

In this lesson, you will learn how to make suggestions, give advice, and express your opinions in various situations. You will also explore modal verbs such as "should," "ought to," and "had better."

Key Vocabulary:

  • Should
  • Ought to
  • Had better
  • Advice
  • Suggestion
  • Recommendation

Sample Dialogues:

Dialogue 1:

Person A: What do you think I should do about my job?

Person B: I think you ought to talk to your manager about your concerns.

Dialogue 2:

Person A: I’m not sure if I should go to the party.

Person B: You had better go! It will be fun.

Dialogue 3:

Person A: What’s your recommendation for a good book?

Person B: I suggest you read "The Alchemist." It's a great story!

Dialogue 4:

Person A: Should I take the bus or the train?

Person B: I think you should take the train; it’s faster.

Dialogue 5:

Person A: I need to lose weight. What do you recommend?

Person B: You ought to exercise regularly and eat healthier.

Dialogue 6:

Person A: I’m feeling stressed about my exams.

Person B: You had better take some time to relax and study effectively.

Dialogue 7:

Person A: Should I ask for help with my project?

Person B: Yes, it’s always a good idea to get advice from others.

Dialogue 8:

Person A: What should I do if I feel sick?

Person B: You ought to see a doctor if you’re not feeling well.

Dialogue 9:

Person A: I’m thinking about moving to a new city.

Person B: You had better consider the cost of living before making a decision.

Dialogue 10:

Person A: Should I invest in stocks?

Person B: I recommend doing some research before investing your money.
